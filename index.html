About Me

The rise of Dro Kenji is a testament to the power of the internet. As a senior in high school, I was releasing a string of captivating singles and mixtapes on my SoundCloud. Now, I am preparing to release my debut album on Mira Touch/ Internet Money Records/ 10K Projects, collaborating closely with the label’s team of platinum-certified producers, and hoping to earn some plaques of my own. Being a true child of the digital age, raised on video games and the lo-fi sounds of rap’s online underground, I have a remarkable ability to capture both the apathy and aspiration of my generation in my songs and a spellbinding voice with which to tell these stories. Raised in Summerville, South Carolina, a town outside of Charleston, I mostly kept to myself. "There’s not really a lot going on," I say of my hometown. "Up until I started making music at the end of 10th grade, I really didn’t do shit but play video games and listen to music." My parents put me on to classic ‘90s-era hip-hop and R&B, but some of my earliest music fandom came from an unlikely combination of my two main hobbies: the video game Rock Band.
